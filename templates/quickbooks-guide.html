QuickBooks Online guide

New to QuickBooks Online? Read this first.

A short, plain-English orientation for lawyers and firm staff who have never opened QuickBooks Online before. What PCLaw Migrate puts into QuickBooks, what stays untouched, and where to find each piece after the import.

In one paragraph

QuickBooks Online is the cloud accounting system your firm is moving to. Think of it as the system of record for accounts, transactions, customers, vendors, and reports — the things PCLaw used to hold. PCLaw Migrate copies your historical accounting data from PCLaw into QuickBooks so your bookkeeper or accountant can carry on from there. Nothing is posted to QuickBooks without your explicit confirmation.

What PCLaw Migrate posts into QuickBooks

  • Chart of accounts. Your PCLaw account list, shown with both account number and name. New accounts are only created when you type CREATE ACCOUNTS; accounts that already exist in QuickBooks are matched, not duplicated.
  • Opening balances. When you run the trial balance step, we post a single balancing journal entry as of the day before your cutover date so QuickBooks starts where PCLaw ended.
  • Transaction history (journal entries). Each PCLaw transaction posts as a QuickBooks JournalEntry. Lines carry the account number plus name so the QuickBooks ledger stays readable.
  • Customers and vendors. A/R lines need a customer; A/P lines need a vendor. We create them in QuickBooks if they don’t exist yet, or reuse the matching record if they do.

What does not happen automatically

PCLaw Migrate is a careful copy step. It does not replace the judgement of a bookkeeper or accountant.

  • It does not replace accountant or bookkeeper review.
  • It does not connect or reconcile QuickBooks bank feeds.
  • It does not change, delete, or archive your old PCLaw data.
  • It does not bypass QuickBooks’ own confirmation gates — you will see typed-confirmation prompts on every posting step.
  • Trust transactions are never auto-posted. We show a reconciliation only; posting trust activity is a per-matter decision your firm makes manually.

Where to find things in QuickBooks after the import

Menu names occasionally change in QuickBooks Online. If your screen doesn’t match the wording below, look for the closest label — the underlying area is the same.

Chart of accounts

Open the Settings gear (top right) and choose Chart of accounts. Each row shows the account number, name, type, and current balance. Sort by number to see them in the same order as your PCLaw export.

Journal entries (your imported transactions)

Use the magnifying-glass search at the top of QuickBooks and type a transaction number, or open the Reports area and run Journal or Transaction Detail by Account. Each PCLaw transaction appears as a single JournalEntry with its original transaction_id in the memo line.

Customers and vendors

Customers live under the Sales or Customers area; vendors live under Expenses or Vendors. Names created by the import appear there with no profile fields filled in — your bookkeeper can add billing details later.

Reports and balance checks

Open the Reports area. The three you’ll use most after a migration:

  • Trial Balance — compare against your PCLaw closing TB.
  • Balance Sheet — sanity-check asset / liability totals.
  • General Ledger — per-account drill-down to spot anything unexpected.

After import — a short checklist

  1. Open the Chart of accounts in QuickBooks and confirm the new accounts look right (number, name, type). Compare against your PCLaw account list.
  2. Spot-check a handful of journal entries — pick a few transaction IDs from the import receipt and search for them in QuickBooks.
  3. Compare balances. Run a Trial Balance in QuickBooks as of your cutover date and compare against the PCLaw closing TB you uploaded.
  4. Invite your bookkeeper or accountant to QuickBooks so they can review and continue from there. Trust and bank-feed reconciliation are theirs to finish.
  5. If something looks wrong, return to the job page in PCLaw Migrate — the Reverse option posts offsetting entries to undo the import.
