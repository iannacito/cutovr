Coverage

More than just the General Ledger.

Law-firm books in PCLaw are not one file. {{ app_name }} is built for the accounting data your firm actually has to bring across — not just a single GL export.

General Ledger

Importable. Posted journal activity for the period you choose, mapped onto your QuickBooks chart and written as JournalEntry records after explicit confirmation.

Chart of Accounts

Preview only. Parsed and compared against your QBO chart to show matches and would-be-creates side-by-side. No QBO accounts are created automatically.

Trial Balance

Validation only. Parsed, totaled, and flagged if debits do not equal credits. Used to reconcile a posted GL import — never auto-posted to QuickBooks.

Bank balances

Used to spot-check operating and trust bank totals during reconciliation. Posting bank journal lines is part of the General Ledger import.

Trust listings

Validation only. Parsed by client / matter with trust totals per bank account, for reconciliation against QBO trust liability and trust bank balances. Never auto-posted to QuickBooks.

Client & matter A/R

Customer entities are auto-created in QuickBooks when a GL row posts to Accounts Receivable. Dedicated A/R aging import is on the roadmap.

Vendor A/P

Vendor entities are auto-created in QuickBooks when a GL row posts to Accounts Payable. Dedicated A/P aging import is on the roadmap.

Cutover reconciliation

Side-by-side checks at cutover so you can confirm every dollar landed where it should before going live.

Coverage depends on what your PCLaw export contains and the workflow you pick. The onboarding guide walks through what each report should look like.

How it works

From PCLaw export to QuickBooks — in six steps.

The whole flow is in the app. No spreadsheets passed around by email, no consultant on a shared screen.

  1. 01

    Export from PCLaw

    Run the General Ledger (and optional A/R, A/P, trust, trial-balance) report from PCLaw and save as CSV. We provide a sample template if you want to dry-run first.

  2. 02

    Upload to {{ app_name }}

    Sign in, name the migration, and upload the CSV. The file is encrypted before any processing happens.

  3. 03

    Validate & map accounts

    We parse the file, run preflight checks (balanced entries, valid accounts, sane dates), and present an account-mapping screen so you confirm every PCLaw account lands where you want in QuickBooks.

  4. 04

    Connect QuickBooks

    Sign in with Intuit. We use Intuit's official OAuth flow — we never see your QuickBooks password, and the connection can be revoked from inside QuickBooks at any time.

  5. 05

    Review & import

    Confirm the QuickBooks company you're posting to, type IMPORT to confirm, and watch journal entries land. Progress and any errors stream back as it runs.

  6. 06

    Verify — or reverse

    Run a one-click verification against QuickBooks. If something looks wrong, one-click reversal posts offsetting REV- entries and you can re-run.
