Why we scope first

Every firm’s books are a little different. A few things shape the work involved, so they shape your price too:

  • How far back you want to go — current year only, or several years of history.
  • The quality of your reports — clean exports move faster than ones that need repair.
  • Client trust accounting — firms holding money in trust need extra care and reconciliation.
  • The overall size of the migration — the volume of accounts, transactions, and files.

Rather than guess with a one-size-fits-all package, we look at your firm on a short call and quote you a clear price at the end of it.

Questions

Plain English answers.

Why don’t you list prices?

Because the right number depends on your firm — how much history you want to move, how clean your reports are, and whether you handle client trust money. We scope all of that on a short discovery call and give you a clear, fixed price before any work begins.

What if I am not sure how far back to go?

That’s exactly what the discovery call is for. We’ll talk through how much history is useful for your firm and you can decide with our guidance — nothing is locked in beforehand.

Do I need to know which reports to upload?

No. We tell you which files help most and walk you through each one. If something is missing or looks off, we flag it before anything is sent to QuickBooks.

Does this include QuickBooks setup?

You’ll need an active QuickBooks Online subscription. Once you do, we handle connecting it to {{ app_name }}, matching your accounts, and posting your history. We don’t sell QuickBooks itself.

What happens after the discovery call?

If the scoped price works for you, you upload your PCLaw reports and answer a few simple questions — that’s your part. From there the guided workflow handles the accounting-heavy steps: we prepare your data, match it to QuickBooks, and walk you through a review before anything posts. If anything looks wrong, the migration can be reversed in one click.
